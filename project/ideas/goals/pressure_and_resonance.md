# Pressure And Resonance

## Core idea

Need and opportunity are not the same thing.

- pressure: how much a goal currently needs attention
- resonance: how naturally the current context affords useful progress

## Reframed for agent-internal goals

Internal agent work often benefits from this split:

- high pressure + low resonance: keep the issue live, but do not derail the current task unless necessary
- low pressure + high resonance: take the cheap win now
- high pressure + high resonance: bring it forward immediately
- low pressure + low resonance: let it fade out

## Why reusable

This avoids one flat priority number. It lets the agent distinguish urgent work from merely timely work, and both from opportunistic work.

## Research roots

This note keeps one directly grounded concept and one design extension:

- [Carver, C. S., & Scheier, M. F. (1982). *Control theory: A useful conceptual framework for personality-social, clinical, and health psychology*.](https://doi.org/10.1037/0033-2909.92.1.111) Retained here as the main source for pressure as discrepancy-driven need for correction.
- [Hayes-Roth, B. (1985). *A blackboard architecture for control*.](https://doi.org/10.1016/0004-3702(85)90063-3) Retained here as the closest root for the agenda-facing side of resonance: what the current context makes newly actionable or worth surfacing.
