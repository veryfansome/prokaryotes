# Failure Generates Epistemic Work

## Core idea

Failure should not only lower confidence or mark a goal as degraded. It should often produce a new, narrower goal: understand the failure well enough to recover.

## Reframed for agent-internal goals

This concept is more native to agent-self-goals than to user-goal tracking. When the agent hits a problem, useful follow-up often looks like:

- identify the blocking assumption
- localize the failure source
- restore missing observability
- find the smallest next experiment

The point is not merely to say "the goal is off track." The point is to create a repair-oriented epistemic objective with its own lifecycle.

## Why reusable

This turns failure into structured control rather than vague degradation. It is a strong candidate for the center of an internal-goals architecture.

## Research roots

- [Parr, T., & Friston, K. J. (2017). *Uncertainty, epistemics and active inference*.](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5721148/) Retained here because failure often reveals an epistemic gap before it reveals a direct next action.
- [Doyle, J. (1979). *A Truth Maintenance System*.](https://doi.org/10.1016/0004-3702(79)90008-0) Retained here for the idea that recovery often begins by finding which supporting assumption broke.
